Chapter I
History
Layers of time in the steppe
1824
A Fortress on the Steppe
Long before the city bore its name, this land was a crossroads — where nomadic routes met, where Russian imperial outposts marked the edge of their reach. The steppe held its silence, and the first settlers listened carefully.
First recorded settlement in the region

1939
Ерейментау is Named
The town officially receives its name — derived from the Kazakh words meaning "twin mountains." The surrounding landscape of granite hills gave the place its identity long before any map was drawn.
Population: approximately 3,000

1970s
The Soviet Chapter
Industry arrived. Collective farms expanded. The population grew. Soviet-era architecture, much of it still standing, shaped the streets. A certain gray poetry settled into the city's bones.
Industrial growth — mining & agriculture

Today
Still Here
Around 12,000 people call Ereymentau home. The old buildings lean against the new. The steppe remains. People remember. Stories persist. That's what this documentary is about.
Administrative center of Ereymentau District

Day 3 · Ereymentau
We arrived when the steppe was already turning gold. The kind of gold that doesn't photograph well — it's in the air, not the grass. Our cameras felt inadequate. So we walked instead. Just walked.
